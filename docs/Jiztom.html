About

Hi! I'm Jiztom Kavalakkatt Francis, a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at Iowa State University.

Problem-solving and innovation fuel my passion for technology. I constantly question how things work and explore the latest advancements. This curiosity led me to focus my Ph.D. on Machine Learning and Explainable AI.

My research dives into quantifying data relevance and how understanding complex data relationships can improve prediction accuracy and explainability in regression models. This approach aims to uncover hidden patterns and non-linear relationships in the world around us.

When I'm not tackling tech challenges, you'll likely find me combining my love for nature and technology. I enjoy exploring the world with a bird's-eye view using my drones, accessing hard-to-reach areas. My passion for camping and 3D printing allows me to create custom tools and gear for personalized experiences.

My current project focuses on developing explainers for regression data sets, aiming to make Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) more transparent and understandable.

I believe in a "no-nonsense" approach and embrace the philosophy of lifelong learning. My motto? "We learn something new every day."

In three words, I'm a Data Scientist, Tinkerer, and Hardware Engineer.

Iowa State University
Pattern Based Multivariable Regression using deep Learning
CVPR 2022

We propose a deep learning methodology for multivariate regression that is based on pattern recognition that triggers fast learning over sensor data. We used a conversion of sensors-to-image, which enables us to take advantage of Computer Vision architectures and training processes. In addition to this data preparation methodology, we explore the use of state-of-the-art architectures to generate regression outputs to predict agricultural crop continuous yield information. Finally, we compare with some top models reported in MLCAS2021. We found that using a straightforward training process, we were able to accomplish an MAE of 4.394, RMSE of 5.945, and R^2 of 0.861.
